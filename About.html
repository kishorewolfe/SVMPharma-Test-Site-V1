    General advice

    Even if you feel better, do not miss any doses and complete the entire course of treatment. Stopping the medicine too soon could cause the infection to recur or worsen. Your doctor will choose the overall length of therapy and precise dosage based on the type of infection you have and how well you respond to the medication

    If you are pregnant, expecting to become pregnant, or breastfeeding, tell your doctor before taking the medication. It can be taken with other medicines without harm, although some drugs (such as antacids and alcohol) can alter its functions and exacerbate adverse effects.

    Some medications can make you dizzy or sleepy, so don't drive or do anything else that demands mental focus until you know how this medication affects you. While using this medication, avoid consuming alcohol since it can exacerbate dizziness.

    If you are allergic to any of the ingredients in a drug, you should avoid taking it. Rarely, a serious allergic reaction may occur, necessitating immediate medical intervention. These signs include rash, swelling of the lips, tongue, or face, shortness of breath, or breathing problems. Let your doctor know if you have such a history.
